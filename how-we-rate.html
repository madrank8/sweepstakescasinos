How We Rate

A transparent, 4-criteria scoring system built from published operator terms, aggregated third-party data, and real reader reports. Here is exactly how every sweepstakes casino on this guide earned its score.

Last updated: July 1, 2026

The Short Version

Every sweepstakes casino on this site is scored against four weighted criteria totaling 100 points. Each score is built from three honest sources: our analysis of the operator's published terms, aggregated third-party data (Trustpilot volume/scores/trends and other public review sources), and reader-submitted reports from real players. Our affiliate relationship with an operator does not shift any score.

Straight about our method: We are an independent editorial site, not a mystery-shopping operation. We do not claim to personally register, cash out, and stopwatch every brand. Where a page reports payout speed, that figure comes from the operator's published policy cross-checked against aggregated user reports — clearly labelled and dated — never a fabricated "we tested it" number.

Sites with unresolved legal action, a persistent pattern of unexplained non-payment in the public record, or terms so opaque we cannot assess them are left off our rankings.

Our Evidence — Where Every Score Comes From

Every claim on a review maps to one of three sources, and we label which:

  • Published operator terms. We read each brand's official terms, bonus rules, cashier, and redemption policy, and record what they actually say — with the date we last verified them.
  • Aggregated third-party data. We structure public signals — Trustpilot review volume, score and trend, plus other established review sources — into our own comparisons rather than quoting a single number out of context.
  • Reader reports. Real players tell us what actually happened — redemption method, amount band, request and payout dates, KYC experience. We moderate submissions, then aggregate them ("median payout across N reader reports, as of [date]"). See the reader-report section below.

How Each Score Is Built

1. Redemption Terms & Speed (30%)

The single most important criterion, and the one most casinos game in their marketing. We do not simply repeat an operator's "average" time. Instead we compare:

  • The redemption window the operator publishes for each method (e-wallet, bank, gift card, crypto), plus minimums, fees, and caps.
  • Aggregated user-reported timings from Trustpilot and reader submissions, including the spread — not just the best case.
  • Whether the terms are clear, and whether there is any pattern of withheld or delayed payouts in the public record.

Brands with opaque redemption terms, or a persistent pattern of non-payment in the public record, are marked down heavily or left off the rankings entirely.

2. Sign-Up Bonus Value (25%)

Marketing inflates bonuses with promotional Gold Coins, locked-up Sweeps Coins, or heavy playthrough. We score the realistic claimable value — what an average player can actually redeem after meeting the playthrough on a 96% RTP slot — and factor in how many steps stand between you and the advertised number. A 1× playthrough beats a bigger bonus locked behind 5×. Maximum redemption caps and time limits are factored in.

3. Ongoing Free Value (25%)

The welcome offer is one-time; what keeps a balance alive is recurring free SC. We map each brand's ongoing no-purchase paths:

  • Daily login streaks and time-based faucets
  • Social-media drops and community giveaways
  • Referral SC and loyalty/VIP top-ups
  • The mail-in AMOE route and how workable it actually is

4. Trust & Support (20%)

This criterion folds together everything that determines whether a brand can be trusted with your time and identity:

  • Operator & licensing transparency — who runs it, disclosed clearly and verifiable.
  • Security & fairness — SSL, RNG-tested studios, and the quality of the games/software providers behind the lobby.
  • KYC & terms fairness — documents required, published verification turnaround, and red-flag clauses (low redemption caps, short dormancy windows, wagering above 1× on welcome SC, "sole discretion" payout language).
  • Support & sentiment — channels offered, published availability, and the balance of positive-to-negative themes across each brand's full review history, not cherry-picked quotes.

Why Some Sites Aren't Listed

You will notice some heavily advertised sweepstakes casinos missing from our top lists. The most common reasons:

  • Persistent non-payment pattern. A consistent, unresolved record of players reporting non-payment across third-party sources keeps a brand off our rankings.
  • Opaque or unfair terms. If the terms are too vague to assess, or contain the red-flag clauses above, we don't rank the brand.
  • Operator pressure. If an operator pressures us to remove documented negative information, we decline the relationship and stop accepting their referral fees.
  • Legal/regulatory issues. Sites with active state cease-and-desist orders or pending consumer-protection litigation are removed until resolved.

Review Refresh Schedule

We re-verify each listed brand's published terms and re-aggregate its third-party and reader data on a rolling basis, and more often for the brands most people read. Every review shows the date it was last verified, so you always know how current the information is. A brand can move up or down between refreshes if reader reports or operator changes warrant it.
