A transparent, 7-criteria scoring system built from published operator terms, aggregated third-party data, and real reader reports. Here is exactly how every sweepstakes casino on this guide earned its score.

Last updated: July 1, 2026

The Short Version

Every sweepstakes casino on this site is scored against seven fixed criteria totaling 100 points. Each score is built from three honest sources: our analysis of the operator's published terms, aggregated third-party data (Trustpilot volume/scores/trends and other public review sources), and reader-submitted reports from real players. Our affiliate relationship with an operator does not shift any score.

Straight about our method: We are an independent editorial site, not a mystery-shopping operation. We do not claim to personally register, cash out, and stopwatch every brand. Where a page reports payout speed, that figure comes from the operator's published policy cross-checked against aggregated user reports — clearly labelled and dated — never a fabricated "we tested it" number.

Sites with unresolved legal action, a persistent pattern of unexplained non-payment in the public record, or terms so opaque we cannot assess them are left off our rankings.

Our Evidence — Where Every Score Comes From

Every claim on a review maps to one of three sources, and we label which:

  • Published operator terms. We read each brand's official terms, bonus rules, cashier, and redemption policy, and record what they actually say — with the date we last verified them.
  • Aggregated third-party data. We structure public signals — Trustpilot review volume, score and trend, plus other established review sources — into our own comparisons rather than quoting a single number out of context.
  • Reader reports. Real players tell us what actually happened — redemption method, amount band, request and payout dates, KYC experience. We moderate submissions, then aggregate them ("median payout across N reader reports, as of [date]"). See the reader-report section below.

How Each Score Is Built

1. Redemption Speed (25 points)

The single most important criterion, and the one most casinos game in their marketing. We do not simply repeat an operator's "average" time. Instead we compare:

  • The redemption window the operator publishes for each method (e-wallet, bank, gift card, crypto).
  • Aggregated user-reported timings from Trustpilot and reader submissions, including the spread — not just the best case.
  • Any minimums, fees, and caps that affect how quickly a real player can actually cash out.

Scoring band (published policy weighted by how consistently reader/third-party reports match it): 0–24 hours = full 25 points. 24–72 hours = 20. 72 hours – 1 week = 12. 1–2 weeks = 5. More than 2 weeks, or a persistent pattern of non-payment in the public record = 0.

2. Welcome Bonus Value (15 points)

Marketing materials inflate bonuses with promotional Gold Coins, locked-up Sweeps Coins, or impossible playthrough requirements. We score the realistic claimable value — what an average player can actually redeem after meeting the playthrough on a 96% RTP slot. Maximum redemption caps and time limits are factored in.

3. Game Library (15 points)

Quality matters more than raw count. A site with 4,000 white-label slots scores lower than a site with 1,500 well-curated titles from Pragmatic Play, Hacksaw, NetEnt, Relax Gaming, and similar regulated providers. Bonus points for:

  • Live dealer integrations (Evolution, Pragmatic Live)
  • Published RTP per game
  • Exclusive titles unavailable elsewhere
  • Working demo/free-play mode

4. KYC & Onboarding Friction (15 points)

Identity verification is a legal requirement, not a flaw — but how it's implemented separates good operators from bad. We assess:

  • Documents the operator requires (more than ID + selfie + proof of address = friction)
  • Published turnaround time (under 24 hours = full points)
  • How players report false-rejection handling — does support actually help, or auto-deny?
  • Whether KYC is required at registration, at first redemption, or only above a threshold

5. Customer Support (10 points)

We record which support channels a brand offers (live chat, email, phone), their published availability, and what players consistently report about response time and resolution quality in third-party reviews and reader reports.

6. Terms & Conditions Fairness (10 points)

Red-flag clauses we look for:

  • Maximum redemption caps below $5,000 per request
  • Dormancy clauses that void SC after <180 days
  • Wagering requirements above 1× on welcome SC
  • "At our sole discretion" language attached to redemption decisions
  • Recent (within 12 months) changes that hurt existing players

7. Platform Stability (10 points)

Assessed via mobile UX on iOS/Android, a security header review (HSTS, CSP, etc.), and a search of public breach databases and outage reports. A history of unplanned 4+ hour outages or any unresolved security incident significantly reduces the score.

Why Some Sites Aren't Listed

You will notice some heavily advertised sweepstakes casinos missing from our top lists. The most common reasons:

  • Persistent non-payment pattern. A consistent, unresolved record of players reporting non-payment across third-party sources keeps a brand off our rankings.
  • Opaque or unfair terms. If the terms are too vague to assess, or contain the red-flag clauses above, we don't rank the brand.
  • Operator pressure. If an operator pressures us to remove documented negative information, we decline the relationship and stop accepting their referral fees.
  • Legal/regulatory issues. Sites with active state cease-and-desist orders or pending consumer-protection litigation are removed until resolved.

Review Refresh Schedule

We re-verify each listed brand's published terms and re-aggregate its third-party and reader data on a rolling basis, and more often for the brands most people read. Every review shows the date it was last verified, so you always know how current the information is. A brand can move up or down between refreshes if reader reports or operator changes warrant it.
