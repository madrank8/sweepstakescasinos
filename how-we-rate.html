How Each Score Is Built

1. Redemption Speed (25 points)

The single most important criterion, and the one most casinos game in their marketing. We do not trust operator-published "average" times. Instead, we measure ours:

  • Submit a redemption request at a known time after KYC is complete.
  • Time-stamp every status change (pending → approved → processing → paid).
  • Repeat across at least three redemption methods (PayPal, ACH, gift card).
  • Repeat the entire test at three different days/times within a quarter.

Scoring band: 0–24 hours = full 25 points. 24–72 hours = 20. 72 hours – 1 week = 12. 1–2 weeks = 5. More than 2 weeks, or any failed redemption = 0 (and the brand is automatically delisted).

2. Welcome Bonus Value (15 points)

Marketing materials inflate bonuses with promotional Gold Coins, locked-up Sweeps Coins, or impossible playthrough requirements. We score the realistic claimable value — what an average player can actually redeem after meeting the playthrough on a 96% RTP slot. Maximum redemption caps and time limits are factored in.

3. Game Library (15 points)

Quality matters more than raw count. A site with 4,000 white-label slots scores lower than a site with 1,500 well-curated titles from Pragmatic Play, Hacksaw, NetEnt, Relax Gaming, and similar regulated providers. Bonus points for:

  • Live dealer integrations (Evolution, Pragmatic Live)
  • Published RTP per game
  • Exclusive titles unavailable elsewhere
  • Working demo/free-play mode

4. KYC & Onboarding Friction (15 points)

Identity verification is a legal requirement, not a flaw — but how it's implemented separates good operators from bad. We score:

  • Documents required (more than ID + selfie + proof of address = friction)
  • Turnaround time (under 24 hours = full points)
  • False-rejection handling (does support actually help, or auto-deny?)
  • Whether KYC is required at registration, at first redemption, or only above a threshold

5. Customer Support (10 points)

We open three real support tickets per review cycle: one trivial ("Where is my SC balance?"), one urgent ("My redemption is stuck"), and one complaint ("Why was my account flagged?"). We score response time, resolution quality, and whether the agent escalated when they should have.

6. Terms & Conditions Fairness (10 points)

Red-flag clauses we look for:

  • Maximum redemption caps below $5,000 per request
  • Dormancy clauses that void SC after <180 days
  • Wagering requirements above 1× on welcome SC
  • "At our sole discretion" language attached to redemption decisions
  • Recent (within 12 months) changes that hurt existing players

7. Platform Stability (10 points)

Measured via independent uptime monitors over the prior 90 days, mobile UX testing on iOS/Android, security header audit (HSTS, CSP, etc.), and a search of public breach databases. A history of unplanned 4+ hour outages or any unresolved security incident significantly reduces the score.

Why Some Sites Aren't Listed

You will notice some heavily advertised sweepstakes casinos missing from our top lists. The most common reasons:

  • Failed the redemption test. Our policy is strict — no redemption, no listing.
  • Pattern of complaints. Multiple verified player complaints about the same issue (e.g., KYC denials without explanation) automatically trigger a re-investigation. Repeated failures = delisting.
  • Operator pressure. If an operator pressures us to remove negative content or refuses to acknowledge documented player issues, we delist them and stop accepting their referral fees.
  • Legal/regulatory issues. Sites with active state cease-and-desist orders, AGCO bans, or pending consumer-protection litigation are removed until resolved.

Re-testing Schedule

Every listed site is re-tested at least once per quarter (every 90 days). High-traffic sites — top 5 in our rankings — are re-tested every 30 days. A site can move up or down the rankings between scheduled reviews if we receive verified complaints or significant operator changes.

The Affiliate Wall

Our reviewers are not told which operators have signed referral agreements with us until after a review is published. Affiliate-relationship status is, of course, ultimately public — but separating the writing from the deal-making materially reduces unconscious bias. Operators that try to negotiate ranking position as a condition of partnership are permanently delisted.
