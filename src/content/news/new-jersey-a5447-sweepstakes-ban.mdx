**New Jersey effectively closed the door on unlicensed dual-currency sweepstakes casinos** when **Assembly Bill 5447** was signed **August 15, 2025**, taking effect immediately. The law treats certain paid-entry online sweepstakes products as **unlawful gambling** under the New Jersey Constitution — reinforcing a posture the **Division of Gaming Enforcement (DGE)** had already signaled through enforcement pressure on operators.

## Context: NJ already had a licensed iGaming market

Unlike states that had no regulated online casino framework, New Jersey has licensed internet gaming under the **Casino Control Act** since 2013. The DGE treats unlicensed online products offering prizes for chance-based play as outside that market — and sweepstakes operators using dual virtual currencies with redemption were viewed as direct competition operating without a license.

A5447 codifies that stance for the paid-entry sweepstakes variant, aligning statute with enforcement.

## Operator response

Following legislative momentum and DGE pressure, **dual-currency operators geo-blocked New Jersey**. In our review set, **zero of thirteen brands** list NJ as available. Players attempting to register from New Jersey typically hit geo-fencing or terms violations.

Sweepstakes Wiz suppresses **all affiliate bonus CTAs** for New Jersey visitors sitewide — not merely on state pages — consistent with promoter-liability trends in other ban states.

## What New Jerseyans can still use

- **DGE-licensed online casinos and sportsbooks** (21+, geolocated in NJ)
- **Atlantic City retail casinos**
- **New Jersey Lottery**
- **Gold-coin-only social casinos** without Sweeps Coin prize redemption (separate category)
