**Montana was an early mover in the 2025 sweepstakes crackdown** when **Senate Bill 555** was signed **May 16, 2025** and took effect **October 1, 2025**. The law **expands Montana's illegal internet gambling definition** to encompass dual-currency online sweepstakes-style products — with **felony-level penalties** for serious violations.

## Why Montana acted early

Montana historically prohibited most forms of gambling outside the state lottery and limited charitable gaming. SB 555 closes a perceived loophole: online platforms offering casino-style games with a secondary redeemable currency that functioned like unlicensed internet gambling.

The felony penalty tier signals **strong deterrence against operators and facilitators** — payment processors, platform vendors, and promoters — rather than a focus on prosecuting individual players.

## Operator and player impact

Following enactment, dual-currency sweepstakes operators added or reinforced **Montana geo-blocks**. Brands in our review set — including Hello Millions, McLuck, and Playfame — list Montana among **restricted states**.

Montana residents should not assume gray-area participation is risk-free: operator terms, geo-fencing, and state criminal definitions all point toward **non-availability**.
