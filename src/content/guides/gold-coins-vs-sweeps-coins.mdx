Every sweepstakes casino runs on **two separate virtual currencies**, and confusing them
is the single most common mistake new players make. In short: **Gold Coins are for fun,
Sweeps Coins are for prizes.** This guide explains exactly what each one does, how you get
them, and why the distinction decides how much real value you can actually take out.

## Quick comparison

| | Gold Coins (GC) | Sweeps Coins (SC) |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Play for fun | Promotional entries → prizes |
| **Monetary value** | None | Redeemable for prizes (per rules) |
| **Can you buy it?** | Yes (optional packages) | No — never sold directly |
| **Can you redeem it?** | No | Yes, once conditions are met |
| **How you get it** | Purchase, daily bonuses, free play | Free bonus with GC purchases, daily login, social drops, mail-in |

## Gold Coins (GC) — the play currency

Gold Coins are a **play-only** currency with **no monetary value**. You use them to enjoy
slots and table games in "fun" mode. You can optionally buy more Gold Coin packages, but
they can never be redeemed for cash or prizes — they are purely entertainment. When your
Gold Coin balance runs out, most casinos top you up for free on a daily basis.

## Sweeps Coins (SC) — the prize currency

Sweeps Coins are the **promotional entry** currency. This is the one that matters for
value: accumulated Sweeps Coins can be **redeemed for prizes** — cash or gift cards —
under each operator's official rules once you meet the minimum threshold and complete
identity verification (KYC).

Crucially, **you never buy Sweeps Coins directly.** They arrive as a *free bonus* attached
to Gold Coin purchases, and — because the model is legally a sweepstakes — through a
mandatory **no-purchase method** such as a daily login bonus, social-media giveaway, or a
postal mail-in request.

## The rule that trips people up

Two things confuse almost everyone at first:

1. **You cannot convert Gold Coins into Sweeps Coins.** They are entirely separate wallets.
   Buying a Gold Coin package may *include* bonus Sweeps Coins, but the Gold Coins
   themselves never become redeemable.
2. **Not all Sweeps Coins are instantly redeemable.** Sweeps Coins won from a *bonus*
   usually must be **played through once (1x)** before they can be redeemed. Sweeps Coins
   won from gameplay are typically redeemable once you hit the operator's minimum. Good
   operators show both balances clearly; the playthrough multiple is one of the things our
   [rating methodology](/how-we-rate/) weighs.

## Why the difference decides where your money goes

Because Gold Coins are worthless for redemption, the **real value** of any welcome offer
or promotion lives entirely in its **Sweeps Coins** — after the playthrough requirement and
the minimum redemption threshold. A headline of "1,000,000 Gold Coins" tells you nothing
about payout potential; the "+ 2 free SC" beside it is what counts.
