Redeeming [Sweeps Coins](/guides/sweeps-coins-explained/) for cash or gift cards is
straightforward once you understand the prerequisites. This guide walks through the whole
process — minimums, playthrough, KYC, methods, and timelines — so your first redemption goes
smoothly.

## Before you can redeem: three prerequisites

1. **Meet the minimum.** Operators set a minimum redeemable-SC threshold, commonly **50 SC
   for gift cards** and **100 SC for cash**.
2. **Clear any playthrough.** Sweeps Coins won from a *bonus* usually must be **played
   through once (1x)** before they become redeemable. SC won from gameplay is typically
   redeemable directly. (More on this in
   [Sweeps Coins explained](/guides/sweeps-coins-explained/).)
3. **Complete identity verification (KYC).** You'll need to verify your identity before a
   payout — usually a government ID, sometimes a selfie and proof of address.

## Step by step

1. **Accumulate redeemable Sweeps Coins** through gameplay until you're above the minimum.
2. **Verify your identity early.** Submit KYC documents as soon as you register — doing this
   *before* your first redemption request avoids the most common delay.
3. **Open the cashier / redeem section** and choose "Redeem" (not "Buy").
4. **Enter the amount** of redeemable SC and select a payout method.
5. **Submit and wait** for the operator to review and process the request.

## Payout methods (vary by operator)

- **E-wallets** — Skrill, PayPal (often the fastest).
- **Bank transfer / ACH** — direct to your account; typically a few business days.
- **Gift cards** — frequently the lowest minimum and quick delivery.
- **Crypto** — offered by some operators.

Each brand supports a different mix; our reviews list the available methods and the
published minimums per operator.

## Typical timelines and why redemptions get delayed

Redemption speed depends on the method and the operator. In general, e-wallets and gift
cards clear faster than bank transfers, and **first-time redemptions take longer** while KYC
is reviewed. Common causes of delay:

- KYC still pending, or a proof-of-address document rejected (use a bank statement PDF, not
  a screenshot, and make sure your ID matches your registered address).
- Weekend or holiday processing gaps.
- A first-time or unusually large redemption triggering a manual review.
