## What is a social casino?

**A social casino is an online platform where you play casino-style games for free, purely
for entertainment. You use a play-money currency — commonly Gold Coins — that has no
monetary value and cannot be exchanged for cash or prizes. The appeal is the games and the
social features (leaderboards, gifting, events), not winning money.**

Think of it like a mobile game with a Vegas theme: the "chips" only unlock more play. Popular
pure social casino brands include titles such as Big Fish Casino, Slotomania, DoubleDown
Casino, House of Fun, Jackpot Party and Zynga's casino games. None of them redeem coins for
money, because they were never designed to — they monetize through optional coin purchases,
not payouts.

## How social casinos work (Gold Coins only)

**You register for free, receive a starting stack of Gold Coins, and play slots or table
games in Gold Coin mode. When coins run low, most apps top you up free on a timer, and you
can optionally buy more for extended play. Gold Coins never convert to cash — they only ever
buy more entertainment, which is the entire model.**

Three things define the pure social model:

- **One currency, no cash value.** Gold Coins (or the app's equivalent) are the only currency,
  and they can't be redeemed for anything.
- **Free top-ups.** Daily bonuses and timers keep you playing without spending.
- **Optional purchases for fun, not profit.** You can buy more coins, but since they can't be
  cashed out, a purchase only extends play — never an investment.

Contrast that with the dual-currency sweepstakes model, where a *second* currency
([Sweeps Coins](/guides/sweeps-coins-explained/)) exists specifically so prizes are possible.

## Social casino vs sweepstakes casino: what's the difference?

**The difference is redemption. A social casino has a single play-only currency and pays no
prizes — you play for fun and stop there. A sweepstakes casino runs two currencies: Gold
Coins for fun and Sweeps Coins that are redeemable for cash or gift cards under published
rules. If a platform lets you cash out, it's a sweepstakes casino, not a social one.**

This is where most "social casino" content misleads readers. Big listicles routinely file
brands like Crown Coins, McLuck and Stake.us under "social casinos" — but those are
**sweepstakes casinos**, because their Sweeps Coins can be redeemed. The label matters: it's
the difference between a game you can only play and a promotion you can potentially cash out
of. We keep the two straight throughout this site, and our
[best sweepstakes casinos](/best/sweepstakes-casinos/) hub only ranks the redeemable kind.

## Can you win real money at a social casino?

**No. At a genuine social casino you cannot win real money — the Gold Coins you play with
have no cash value and are never redeemable, by design. The only way to turn free casino-style
play into a real prize in the US is at a sweepstakes casino, and only through Sweeps Coins,
after you clear playthrough, hit the redemption minimum, and pass identity verification.**

If real prizes are your goal, you want the sweepstakes model, not a pure social app. Even then,

## Are social casinos legal?

**Yes, in nearly every US state. Because pure social casinos award no prizes and their coins
have no cash value, they're treated as entertainment apps rather than gambling, so they face
few of the restrictions aimed at real-money or redeemable play. The wave of 2025–26 state laws
targets the redeemable sweepstakes model — not play-for-fun social games.**

That's an important nuance: when a headline says a state "banned social casinos," it almost
always means the **sweepstakes** (dual-currency, redeemable) model. Pure social apps generally
remain available. Because the redeemable model is what's actually restricted, always check the
current picture on our [state legality hub](/state-legality/) before assuming a Sweeps Coins
brand is available where you live — and note that we suppress offers in banned states ourselves.

## Free social casino games: what you'll actually play

**The games are the same casino-style titles you'd expect: online slots are by far the biggest
category, followed by video poker, blackjack, roulette, baccarat and bingo. At a sweepstakes
casino these same titles are playable free in Gold Coin mode, with an optional Sweeps Coins
mode layered on top for prize-eligible play.**

Because both models license games from the same studios, the *game experience* is nearly
identical — the difference is purely whether a redeemable currency exists. So "free social
casino games" and "free Gold Coin play at a sweepstakes casino" look and feel the same; only
the sweepstakes version gives you a path to a prize.

## How do social casinos make money?

**Social casinos make money primarily through optional in-app purchases — players buy extra
virtual currency, premium packages or special items to extend play. Advertising, partnerships
and sponsorships add further revenue. Buying anything is always optional, and at sweepstakes
casinos those optional Gold Coin purchases come bundled with free Sweeps Coins.**

This is why the games are free to start: the business model is a small share of players
choosing to buy more entertainment, not a house edge on cash wagers. It also explains why
generous free coin grants exist — they keep players engaged in a free-to-play loop.
