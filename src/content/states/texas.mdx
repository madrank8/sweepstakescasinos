## What Texas law says about sweepstakes casinos

Texas Penal Code [Chapter 47](https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/PE/htm/PE.47.htm) defines gambling as an agreement to win something of value based on chance **in exchange for consideration**. Sweepstakes operators argue that free Sweeps Coin entry — daily bonuses, mail-in requests, social promotions — means players can participate without consideration, placing the activity outside the gambling definition.

Texas has not enacted a dual-currency ban comparable to California or New York as of 2026. The framework remains the subject of operator terms and general promotional law — not a Texas-specific sweepstakes casino statute.

## What "no purchase necessary" actually means

Every legitimate sweepstakes operator must offer a free path to Sweeps Coins — typically daily login bonuses or postal mail-in requests. You are never required to buy Gold Coin packages to participate. If a site removes free entry, that is a red flag. Read each operator's official sweepstakes rules for eligibility, redemption, and state carve-outs.
