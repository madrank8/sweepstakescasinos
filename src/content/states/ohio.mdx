## Why sweepstakes casinos are different from real-money gambling

Ohio authorizes licensed real-money casino gaming and sports betting through the Ohio Casino Control Commission, but online real-money slots and table games are not broadly available. Sweepstakes casinos sit in a separate category: they use **Gold Coins** (entertainment-only, no cash value) and **Sweeps Coins** distributed through promotions and a mandatory free-entry path, redeemable for prizes under each operator's official rules. ## What Ohio law says about sweepstakes casinos

Ohio's gambling rules live in [Ohio Revised Code Chapter 2915](https://codes.ohio.gov/ohio-revised-code/chapter-2915), which defines "gambling," a "scheme of chance," and related offenses. The classic three-part gambling test asks whether there is a **prize**, an element of **chance**, and **consideration** (something of value risked to participate). Sweepstakes operators contend that a genuine free entry method — daily bonuses, social promotions, or a postal mail-in request — removes the consideration element, so the dual-currency model falls outside these definitions.

As of mid-2026, Ohio has **not** enacted a dedicated dual-currency sweepstakes ban comparable to California's AB-831 or New York's S5935A. The subject has, however, attracted regulatory and legislative attention, and Ohio's gaming regulator has shown a willingness to scrutinize unlicensed online products. Players should treat the landscape as **evolving** and monitor official Ohio Casino Control Commission communications.

> **Direct answer:** Ohio has not banned dual-currency sweepstakes casinos as of mid-2026, and most reviewed operators that accept the state still do — but regulatory scrutiny is rising, so confirm each operator's current terms before you register.
